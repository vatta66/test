# Чем меньше информации, тем лечге построить когерентную картину

- To make your story believable ([[Влияние когерентности истории на убеждение]]), information doesnt necessarily need to be full
- Often the less you know, the easier it is for you to make a [[Ассоциативная когерентность|coherent]] picture and believe in it
	- In science, ex biology, if you dont know a lot of details of some process, its very easy for you to imagine it and believe that you truly understand it: some “particular” structures do some “particular” work using some “particular” enzymes, and as a result we have some behavior or appearance or something - [[Педагогика MOC]]
		- There is a feel of [[Когнитивная легкость]]
		- But when we start to add more details to it, its become controversial because now we need to realize all interactions between those parts, and often its demand a lot of effort
			- The more details, the more effort to understand, the less [[Мотивация]] during [[Мотивационная теория ожиданий]]
				- It correlates with [[Виды структуры изложения содержания учебного процесса]]
	- If you are a newbie, you can be undergone this effect, you start to think that you know and understand a lot - [[Эффект Даннинга-Крюгера]]
		- Popular science can worsen this problem because a lot of people are getting a feel of mastering of just understanding of some hard topics
			- Consequently, they make wrong decisions ([[Принятие решения]])
- If you want to convince someone ([[Способы убеждения]]), dont give him too much information and dont make it too hard
	- Its better not to say everything, than say too hard and with explicit contradictions
